# 100-Point ICP Scorecard Rubric

## Weighted Dimensions

| Dimension | Weight | What it measures | How to score |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pain severity | /25 | How acute is the problem | 5=nice-to-have → 25=blocks growth/breaks workflow |
| Trigger strength | /20 | Is something making it "now" | 0=no trigger → 20=active trigger in last 90 days |
| Willingness to pay | /20 | Can and will they pay our ACV | 5=below floor → 20=priority budget category |
| Reachability | /15 | Can we reach the decision maker | 5=cold-only → 15=warm intros/active community |
| Time to value | /10 | How fast will they see ROI | 2=months → 10=days |
| Strategic fit | /10 | Reference-class win for next segment | 2=nobody-cares → 10=unlocks a segment |

## Tier Cutoffs

- **Tier 1 (Primary ICP):** ≥75 — full sales motion
- **Tier 2 (Secondary):** 55–74 — automated/lighter touch
- **Tier 3 (Stretch):** 40–54 — opportunistic only
- **Anti-ICP:** <40 — do not pursue

## Calibration

These weights are starting defaults. Tune based on data after first 30–50 deals. The framework matters more than the numbers.

Note: This is a house rubric, not a canonical industry framework like MEDDIC. MEDDIC/MEDDPICC are deal-qualification frameworks distinct from this account-fit scorecard.

## Confidence Rubric

| Confidence | Evidence base | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| High | ≥30 won deals OR ≥30 buyer interviews | Data-backed; downstream skills can act without hedging |
| Medium | 10–29 won deals or interviews | Pattern real but small; plan to re-run after 20 more deals |
| Low | 1–9 deals or interviews | Working hypothesis; tag artifacts `#icp-low-confidence` |
| Hypothesis-only | 0 customers, 0 interviews | Theory only; recommend 8–10 buyer interviews before acting |
