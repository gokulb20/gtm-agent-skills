# Buyer / Champion / User / Blocker Role Map

## The Four Roles (after Miller-Heiman Strategic Selling)

| Role | Who they are | What they care about | What kills the deal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Buyer (Economic) | Signs the contract / approves budget | ROI, risk, strategic alignment | Cost, vendor risk, competing priorities |
| Champion | Internal advocate driving the purchase | Career outcome from project succeeding | No quick win; champion leaves company |
| User (End) | Day-to-day operator | Personal time, workflow ease, learning curve | Tool is harder than status quo |
| Blocker | Has veto or delay power (Security/IT/Procurement/Legal) | Compliance, vendor management, risk | Triggered late; gates not met |

For each ICP segment, fill all four. **Skipping the Blocker role is the #1 reason mid-market+ deals stall.**

## Template Per Role

```
=== [ROLE NAME] ===
Title patterns: [e.g., VP of [function], CXO]
Seniority: [VP+ / Director+ / etc.]
Cares about: [3 priorities, in their language]
Kills the deal when: [3 risks]
Where to find: [LinkedIn filter / community / event]
```

## Worked Example — WorkflowDoc (fictional)

**Buyer:** VP/Director of Support, budget $5k–$50k, cares about reducing escalation rate + onboarding new reps faster. Found on LinkedIn Sales Nav + Support Driven Slack.

**Champion:** Support Operations Manager, wants a visible career win in 6–8 weeks. Found on LinkedIn + CX Accelerator.

**User:** Tier 1/2 Support Specialist, wants to resolve tickets faster without asking manager. Kills adoption when authoring takes longer than doing the work.

**Blocker:** IT Security / Procurement (>$10k purchases), cares about SOC 2 Type II, data residency. Kills deal when triggered late. Disarm with security pack pre-built.
