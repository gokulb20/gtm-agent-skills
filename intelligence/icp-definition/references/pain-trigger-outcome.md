# Pain-Trigger-Outcome Chain with Workaround Analysis

## Framework

Echoes Bosworth's Pain Chain from *Solution Selling*; popularized as "trigger event selling" by Craig Elias and Aaron Ross.

## Chain Structure

- **Pain:** the underlying chronic problem (always present)
- **Workaround:** what they do today to limp through — and what that workaround costs
- **Trigger:** the event that turns chronic pain into acute, "fix this now" pain
- **Outcome:** what the buyer's life looks like with the problem solved (in their words)

The Workaround is load-bearing: (1) it IS a competitive alternative in Dunford's sense, and (2) its cost justifies the deal.

## Template

```
PAIN (chronic):
[2–3 sentences in buyer language]

CURRENT WORKAROUND:
[What they do today — DIY / spreadsheet / hire someone / accept it / ignore it]

COST OF WORKAROUND:
- Time: [hours/week or days/quarter]
- Money: [direct $ + opportunity cost]
- Risk: [error rate, security exposure, churn risk]
- Accuracy: [what slips through]
- Reputation: [internal trust loss, external CSAT/NPS impact]

TRIGGERS (acute, last 90 days):
1. [observable event] — Type: need/buy
2. [observable event] — Type: need/buy
...

OUTCOME (90-day dream state):
[What success looks like, in measurable terms, in buyer language]
```

## Worked Example — WorkflowDoc (fictional)

**Pain:** "Our team's knowledge lives in 8 different places — old Slack threads, stale Confluence pages, the Senior Manager's head. New reps escalate things they shouldn't because they can't find or trust what's there."

**Workaround:** Senior reps answer the same 30 questions in Slack each week; new reps DM their manager; Confluence pages are stale within 60 days.

**Cost:** ~6 hrs/week senior rep time (~$45k/yr); risk of unverified workarounds leaking to customers; ~40% of Confluence pages stale by 90 days.

**Triggers:** Support team grew >30% in last 6 months (need); VP of Support hired (need); Series B funded <90 days (buy); annual budget refresh window (buy).

**Outcome:** Reps resolve 25% more tickets at Tier 1; new reps productive in 30 days instead of 90; runbooks update themselves from real tickets.
