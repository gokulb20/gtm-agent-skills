# Signal Taxonomy

## 10 Standard Categories

| Category | Specific signals | Detection difficulty |
|---|---|---|
| Product | Releases, removals, pricing changes, packaging, new categories | Medium |
| Positioning | Homepage hero, deck slide 1, key claim language | Low |
| Pricing | List-price changes, new tiers, discounting patterns | Medium-High |
| Capital | Funding rounds, M&A, layoffs, exec changes | Low |
| Hiring | New leadership (CRO, CMO, CPO), team scale-up areas | Low |
| Customer | New named customer, churn (rare), case study patterns | Medium |
| Channel / GTM | Partnership announcements, integration launches, new geographies | Medium |
| Content | Major thought-leadership pieces, conference talks, podcasts | Low |
| Sentiment | G2 review trends, community chatter, Glassdoor | Medium |
| Win/Loss | Patterns in our deals vs. them | High (internal) |

## Category Selection Per Competitor

Not all 10 matter for every competitor. Pick the 4–6 with highest decision-relevance.

**Direct top-3:** Product + Positioning + Pricing + Hiring + Sentiment + Win/Loss
**Direct others:** Product + Positioning + Pricing
**Substitute:** Pricing + Sentiment

Don't track all 10 for everyone — that's noise.
