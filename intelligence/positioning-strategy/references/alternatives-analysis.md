# Alternatives Analysis — Dunford Component 1

## Framework

Positioning is *relative to alternatives*. Skipping this step is the most common failure mode. A "great" feature is only great relative to what the customer would otherwise use.

## Required Structure

| Alternative | Type (Direct/Indirect/Status quo) | What buyer gets | Trade-off |
|---|---|---|---|

## Rules

- Minimum 4 entries. Status quo is the most common alternative for early-stage products.
- If `icp-definition` produced a Workaround Analysis, each workaround entry becomes an alternative here. The workaround's *cost* becomes the trade-off column.
- Include DIY / spreadsheets / "do nothing" / "hire a person" as alternatives.

## Worked Example — WorkflowDoc (fictional)

| Alternative | Type | What buyer gets | Trade-off |
|---|---|---|---|
| Guru | Direct | Verified knowledge platform; strong integrations | $25k–$80k ACV; AI authoring bolted on; 4–8 week setup |
| Stonly | Direct | Decision-tree authoring | Decision-tree ≠ runbook prose; reactive AI |
| Notion (status quo) | Substitute | Already paid for; everyone uses it | Generic; not support-specific; AI is search/summarize |
| Hire a Knowledge Manager | Status quo | Dedicated headcount writes runbooks | $80–120k salary; people-dependency |
| Do nothing | Status quo | $0 cost | Knowledge stays in heads; new rep ramp stays at 90 days |
