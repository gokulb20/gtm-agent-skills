# For-and-Against Wedges — Per Competitor

## Template

```
vs. [Competitor]
WHEN buyer is: [trait]
WE WIN BECAUSE: [specific factor]
THEY ATTACK US WITH: "[expected critique]"
WE COUNTER WITH: "[response]"
THE QUESTION THAT FLIPS IT: "[discovery question]"
```

## Purpose

Generic positioning fails on contact with deals. This connects positioning directly to sales reality. A positioning skill that doesn't produce this is academic.

## Worked Example — WorkflowDoc (fictional)

### vs. Guru
WHEN buyer is: Series B SaaS, support team <20, mid-market budget
WE WIN BECAUSE: Guru's $25k+ ACV is built for 500+ emp scale. Their AI authoring was bolted on in 2024.
THEY ATTACK US WITH: "We have AI features too, plus 8 years of enterprise trust."
WE COUNTER WITH: "Their AI workflow starts with a prompt; ours starts with your closed tickets."
FLIP QUESTION: "Show me a runbook your team drafted from a real ticket in under 5 minutes."

### vs. Stonly
WHEN buyer is: Wants prose runbooks, not decision trees; budget under $20k
WE WIN BECAUSE: Decision trees work for FAQs; runbook prose is what support engineers need.
THEY ATTACK US WITH: "Decision trees are more interactive."
WE COUNTER WITH: "For complex internal troubleshooting, your team needs prose with branching context."
FLIP QUESTION: "When your senior engineer documents a complex triage workflow, do they draw a flowchart or write a runbook?"

### vs. Notion (status quo)
WHEN buyer is: Notion already deployed company-wide
WE WIN BECAUSE: When pain is acute, generic Notion structure breaks down. We're additive, not replacement.
THEY ATTACK US WITH: "We already pay for Notion."
WE COUNTER WITH: "Notion is your wiki for everyone. We're the runbook system for your support team."
FLIP QUESTION: "How much of your senior support manager's week is spent writing or re-writing Notion pages?"
