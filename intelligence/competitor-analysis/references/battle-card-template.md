# Battle Card Template

## Standard Format (1 page)

```
=== BATTLE CARD: [Competitor Name] ===

THEIR PITCH (one line):
[How they describe themselves, in their words]

THEIR BEST CUSTOMER:
- Segment: [size, industry, geo]
- Use case: [primary]
- Why they buy: [in customer language]

WHERE THEY WIN:
- [segment/use case 1]
- [segment/use case 2]

WHERE THEY LOSE:
- [weakness 1, evidenced]
- [weakness 2, evidenced]

THEIR ATTACK ON US:
- They will say: "[expected attack]"
- Counter: "[our response]"
- Flip question: "[discovery question]"

OUR ATTACK ON THEM:
- Our wedge: [single sentence]
- Discovery question: "[surfaces their weakness]"
- Proof: [case study / data point]

PRICING:
- Model: [seat/usage/flat]
- Public list: [$X]
- Real ACV: [$Y, source]
- Recent changes: [if any]

RECENT MOVES (<90 days):
- [event + date + implication]

WATCH LIST:
- [thing to monitor]
```

## Key Rules

- Every weakness must cite a source (G2 review, customer quote, public source)
- 1 wedge, 1 flip-question, 1 proof — no more
- Date every card; >90 days = flag as stale, recommend `competitive-intelligence` for refresh
- No FUD — unverified attacks lose deals when buyers fact-check
