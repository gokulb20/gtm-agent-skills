# Response Frameworks — Objection Handling

## Feel-Felt-Found
**Origin:** Traditional sales technique; commonly traced to Procter & Gamble training (1980s). No canonical author.
**When to use:** Pure empathy + reframe needed. Acknowledge emotion → reference others → resolve with insight.
**Structure:**
- Feel: "I hear you — that's a real concern."
- Felt: "Others in similar roles felt the same way initially."
- Found: "What they found was [specific insight from ICP P-T-O]."

## Re-discovery (Bosworth Pain-Questioning)
**Origin:** Mike Bosworth — Solution Selling methodology.
**When to use:** Stated objection isn't the real reason. Surface the pain underneath.
**Structure:**
- Acknowledge the surface objection.
- Pain-question: "What happens when [stated problem persists]?"
- Anchor to cost of doing nothing (time/money/risk).

## Time-shift
**When to use:** Bad-timing or not-now with a resume window.
**Structure:**
- Acknowledge timing.
- Lock specific resume window with permission.
- "Would it make sense to reconnect in [specific window]?"

## Counter-position (Dunford)
**Origin:** April Dunford — *Obviously Awesome*.
**When to use:** Objection cites a competitor. Reframe the alternative (not your product vs theirs — DIY / status-quo / build-vs-buy).
**Structure:**
- Acknowledge their current choice.
- Reframe the alternative's trade-off (not "X is bad" — "X optimizes for Y; our users mentioned Z as the gap").
- Offer 5-min comparison or specific evaluation question.

## Variant Generation Rules
- 2–3 variants per objection: light / medium / direct tone
- Each variant ≤80 words
- One CTA per variant
- Must reference verified prior context (never invent)
- Respect channel-isolation per function-3 conventions
