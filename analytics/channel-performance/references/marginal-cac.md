# Marginal CAC Analysis — Channel Performance

## Concept

Marginal CAC = expected cost of acquiring the NEXT deal at current spend level.

Derived from standard microeconomics — the **equimarginal principle (~19th-century)** applied per modern **Marketing Mix Modeling (MMM)** practice.

## Important Attribution

- **Aaron Ross** (*Predictable Revenue*, 2011) popularized outbound specialization + SDR/AE split
- **Mark Roberge** (*Sales Acceleration Formula*, 2015) popularized engineering rigor in sales process
- **Neither owns marginal-CAC** — it's a standard microeconomics concept

## How to Compute

1. Plot each channel's cumulative deals vs cumulative spend
2. The slope at the current point = marginal CAC (cost of the next deal)
3. Compare across channels: lower marginal CAC = better next-dollar return

## Diminishing Returns Position

| Position | Meaning | Marginal CAC |
|---|---|---|
| Headroom | Channel far from capacity | Low — good next-dollar return |
| Near-cap | Channel approaching limits | Rising — diminishing returns |
| At-cap | Channel maxed out | High — next dollar has low return |

## Reallocation Logic

- Channel with low CPD but at-cap → don't double-down (high marginal CAC)
- Channel with higher CPD but headroom → consider investing (low marginal CAC)
- The two rankings (CPD vs marginal CAC) may disagree → surface both
