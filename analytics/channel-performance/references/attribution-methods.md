# Attribution Methods — Channel Performance

## Last-Touch Attribution

- Deal credited to the channel of the last touch before reply/meeting
- **Pros:** Simple, deterministic
- **Cons:** Over-credits the cheapest closing channel (usually email); ignores setup work by other channels

## Multi-Touch Attribution (Weighted)

- Deal credited to ALL touching channels, weighted by position in the journey
- **Weighting:** First 30% / Middle 40% / Last 30% of touches
- **Pros:** Surfaces channels doing setup work that other channels finish
- **Cons:** More complex; requires full touch history

## Surface Both, Don't Pick One

The two methods tell different stories. Always surface both side by side.

Example:
- Last-touch CPD: Email $150 / LinkedIn $233 / Call $480
- Multi-touch CPD: Email $135 / LinkedIn $250 / Call $420

Call looks expensive on last-touch but gets credit for setup work in multi-touch — 7 of 5 deals had call as middle-touch.

## Audience-Channel Confound

When email runs on Tier-1 and cold call runs on Tier-2, the baseline reply rates differ. Note when channel-audience confound exists in the report.
